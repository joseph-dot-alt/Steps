Step 1: Gather Materials

You'll need a container, rubber tubing, a gas collection bag, organic waste, and water.

Step 2: Set Up the Container

Choose a large, airtight container as the main digester. Drill a hole in the lid for the tubing.

Step 3: Add Organic Waste

Fill the container with organic waste like food scraps or animal manure. Add water to cover the waste.

Step 4: Connect Tubing

Attach the rubber tubing to the hole in the lid. This will direct biogas to the gas collection bag.

Step 5: Store and Monitor

Seal the container tightly, store it in a warm place, and monitor gas production after 1-2 weeks.

Creating a biogas digester is a great way to produce renewable energy at home!
